Co Founder and CEO of Microsoft: 1975 - 2000

Bill Gates is the co-founder of Microsoft Corporation, which he started in 1975 along with Paul Allen. As a founder, he played a key role in developing the company’s vision of putting a computer on every desk and in every home. His technical expertise and business strategy helped Microsoft become one of the world’s leading technology companies.

Gates served as Microsoft’s first Chief Executive Officer (CEO). During his time as CEO, he led the company’s growth, especially through the success of products like MS-DOS and the Windows operating system. Under his leadership, Microsoft became a dominant force in the personal computer industry.

In 2000, Bill Gates stepped down as CEO and handed over the position to Steve Ballmer. Although he remained involved with Microsoft for several years in different roles, he gradually shifted his focus toward philanthropy through the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation. Today, he is no longer the CEO but is widely recognized as Microsoft’s co-founder and former leader.

Chief Software Architect: 2000 - 2008

Bill Gates served as Microsoft’s Chief Software Architect after stepping down as CEO in 2000. In this role, he focused primarily on the company’s technical direction rather than day-to-day business operations. He remained deeply involved in reviewing product development, guiding software strategy, and ensuring that Microsoft continued to innovate in areas such as Windows, Office, and internet technologies.

As Chief Software Architect, Gates worked closely with development teams and senior executives to shape the company’s long-term technology vision. His role emphasized maintaining high technical standards and driving innovation within Microsoft’s software platforms.
